MA MUNDESHWARI DEVI TEMPLE


(माँ मुंडेश्वरी देवी मंदिर)


World’s Oldest Functional Temple

Introduction

This Hindu temple is considered the world’s oldest functional temple where rituals have been performed continuously for centuries. Located at the summit of Piwara Hill at a height of about 600 feet, it stands as a remarkable symbol of spiritual heritage.

Spiritual Significance

Maa Mundeshwari Devi Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Shakti, and is located in the Mundeshwari Hills of Bihar. The temple also features idols of Ganesha, Surya, and Vishnu. Dated back to 108 AD by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), it has been a protected monument since 1915 and is considered a fine example of the Nagara style of architecture.

During the 7th century, Shaivism became dominant and the four-faced Chatur Mukhalingam was placed centrally as the presiding deity. Later, the Cheros—a tribal community—revered Shakti, giving Mundeswari (Durga) the prime position. Durga’s image remains in a niche of the temple wall, while the Mukhalingam continues in the central sanctum. Rituals have been carried out here without interruption for centuries. Festivals like Ramnavami and Shivratri attract thousands of pilgrims every year.

Cultural Heritage

The region surrounding the temple is a rich center of ancient Indian culture, known for early forms of music, art, crafts, and script (lipi).

Additional Information

The temple is located in Kaimur, Bihar. Nearby attractions include Maa Mundeshwari Eco Park. Nearest railway stations are Bhabhua Road (BBU), DDU, and SSM. Closest towns include Bhabhua, Bhagwanpur, and Mohania.
